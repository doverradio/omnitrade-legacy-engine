# EVIDENCE_LAYER.md

## OmniTrade Legacy Engine - Evidence Layer

### Status: Tier-1 Architectural Specification

The Evidence Layer defines how OmniTrade preserves trustworthy production decision history as immutable, replayable evidence. It is the contract between the Execution layer (which acts) and the Learn layer (which studies, scores, and advises).

This document is intentionally architecture-first and implementation-resilient. It explains why this layer exists, what boundaries it enforces, and which invariants future contributors must preserve.

---

## Evidence Layer

### Purpose

Production is authoritative.

The Learn layer never invents history, reconstructs hand-wavy narratives, or treats telemetry as decision truth. It consumes immutable production evidence and only that evidence.

The Evidence Layer exists so every downstream subsystem can ask rigorous questions using trustworthy records:

- What did the system decide?
- Why did it decide that?
- What actually happened?
- What should be learned from that outcome?

Without immutable evidence, Replay, Decision Quality, AI Coach, Decision Intelligence, and Capital Allocation become opinion systems. With immutable evidence, they become accountable systems.

---

### Guiding Principles

1. Production is authoritative.
2. Evidence is immutable once persisted.
3. Replay consumes evidence; it never rewrites history.
4. AI consumes evidence; it never fabricates outcomes.
5. Research never modifies production state.
6. Human approval governs promotion decisions.
7. Every important decision should eventually become replayable.
8. Telemetry can explain system health; only evidence explains decision truth.

### Canonical Evidence Objects

#### Signal

A prediction or intent produced by strategy logic.

- Indicates potential action.
- Is not execution evidence.

#### Risk Event

A risk evaluation outcome for a decision candidate.

- Represents approval, rejection, resize, or guardrail behavior.
- Is not execution evidence.

#### Paper Trade Event

Canonical definition (repository source of truth):

A persisted row in `trades` with `is_paper=true`, representing one simulated execution fill event associated with a `paper_account_id` and `executed_at` timestamp.

This is the canonical definition for dashboard, Replay, AI Coach, Decision Quality, Decision Intelligence, and Capital Allocation.

#### Decision Record

Immutable production decision memory.

- Captures rationale, context, confidence, and risk outcome.
- Exists whether a trade was executed or not.

#### Decision Package

Immutable replay artifact assembled from production evidence.

- Deterministic input bundle for replay and analysis.

#### Replay Evidence

Read-only output generated by replay runs.

- Never alters production evidence.

#### Decision Quality

Evidence-based scoring of decision quality.

- Evaluates decisions; does not execute decisions.

#### AI Coach Observation

Deterministic advisory interpretation of evidence.

- Observational and explanatory, never autonomous execution.

#### Decision Intelligence Recommendation

Synthesized recommendation from accumulated evidence.

- Advisory output for humans and governance flows.

#### Capital Allocation Recommendation

Suggested paper capital distribution based on evidence quality and consistency.

- Recommendation only, not direct allocation authority.

#### Strategy Promotion

Human-approved production change.

- Final governance step before production strategy changes.

---

### Evidence vs Telemetry

Telemetry and evidence are both useful; they are not interchangeable.

#### Telemetry (system measurement)

Examples:

- worker heartbeat
- ingestion counts
- executions attempted
- queue depth

Telemetry measures whether the system is running.

#### Evidence (decision measurement)

Examples:

- signals
- paper trade events
- decision records
- decision packages

Evidence measures what the system decided and what happened.

Rule:

- Telemetry can trigger investigation.
- Evidence resolves investigation.

### Evidence Lifecycle

```text
Decision
  |
Persist
  |
Package
  |
Replay
  |
Evaluate
  |
Observe
  |
Recommend
  |
Approve
  |

```

Lifecycle interpretation:

- Decision/Persist occur in production context.
- Package creates deterministic replay inputs.
- Replay/Evaluate/Observe/Recommend occur in Learn context.
- Approve/Promote return to governed production change.

### Repository Boundaries

#### Execution Layer

Produces evidence.

- market ingestion
- strategy evaluation
- risk evaluation
- paper execution
- decision record persistence
- decision package production

#### Learn Layer

Consumes evidence.

- replay
- quality scoring
- advisory coaching
- intelligence synthesis
- allocation recommendation

Boundary invariants:

- Execution never depends on Replay.
- Replay never changes production evidence.
- Learn outputs are advisory until human approval.

---

### Repository Invariants

1. Evidence is immutable.
2. Decision Packages are deterministic for the same evidence set.
3. Replay is read-only against production evidence.
4. AI Coach is advisory.
5. Decision Quality scores evidence; it does not alter evidence.
6. Capital Allocation recommends; it does not directly execute.
7. Humans approve strategy promotion.
8. Production remains authoritative.

Any change that violates these invariants is an architectural regression.

---

### Future Evolution

The Evidence Layer allows the platform to expand learning depth without destabilizing production execution.

Natural expansion paths:

- multiple competing replay agents over identical evidence
- AI research over append-only decision corpora
- statistical evaluation and calibration over long-horizon evidence
- portfolio allocation learning on quality-filtered evidence
- continuous learning loops with human approval gates

Crucially, these expansions do not require changing the production decision pipeline. They consume stronger evidence, not different evidence.

---

### Non-Goals

The Evidence Layer does not:

- execute trades
- replay decisions
- optimize strategies
- allocate capital
- promote strategies

Its sole responsibility is preserving trustworthy production evidence so those systems can operate rigorously.

## Core Architectural Principle

Execution produces evidence.

Learning consumes evidence.

Governance decides what returns to production.

Execution systems create immutable production history.

Learn systems analyze that history without modifying it.

Governance, through human approval, is the only path by which learning influences production.

This separation preserves explainability, reproducibility, auditability, safety, and architectural stability.

---

### Operating Summary

The Evidence Layer is OmniTrade's memory contract.

Production acts.
Production records.
Learn systems study immutable evidence.
Humans approve promotion.

This separation is the foundation of safe iteration, explainable learning, and long-term architectural stability.
